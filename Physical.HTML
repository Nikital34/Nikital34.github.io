A Guide on How to Stay Fit Through Exercises with and Without a Gym


Whether you're looking to get into crossfit or gain muscle mass through more traditional work outs, we got you covered with all the information you need!



Starter Home Workouts for Crossfit

There are many different types of workouts you can use to get into crossfitting at home if you don't want to use the gym during these COVID riddled times. We will provide you with some good examples below.



  • The Chipper Workout:100 press ups, 75 bodyweight squats, 50 burpees, 25 push ups (do as many of the following as you can in the fastest time possible)
  • Cardiois an important workout for everyone getting into crossfitting, and therefore you should aim to run or jog outside as many days as possible, typically startng off with 2 mile runs at your own pace, which you can increase overtime!
  • There are numerousworkouts you can do to kick off crossfitting at home. A good start would be to implement the following in your workouts: squats, sit ups, lunges, burpees, plank, mountain climbers, press-ups, hollow rocks, jump squats, and hand-release press-ups


Starter Home Workouts to Put on Muscle Weight

There are numerous workouts you can do at home, if you don't want to go to a public gym. Here are some for each main muscle group.



  • Chest, Triceps, and Shoulders Muscle Groups: Pushups are a great home exercise for building these muscles. They are efficient, and at the same time there are many variations of them.
  • Back and Biceps Muscle Groups: Pulls up are an exercise that will help you bild back and biceps at home. Like with pushups, there are a few variations of these, which is a benefit of the exercise.
  • Abdominal Muscle Groups: There are many ways to help build a strong core with workouts at home. Some of these include mountain climbers, crunches, lying leg raises, and planks.
  • Leg Muscle Groups:Jump squats, split squats, lunges, and pistol squats are all effective exercises to help build leg muscles.


Starter Crossfit Exercises at the Gym

Crossfitting at the gym has even more options than at home. Check them out below.



  • The EMOM:EMOM stands for every minute on the minute, and this workout includes doing three power cleans and jerks every minute for 10 minutes in a row.
  • The AMRAP: AMRAP stands for as many rounds as possible, and this workout focuses on doing 8 front squats and 8 push presses within 12 minutes.
  • The RFT:RFT stands for rounds for time, and this workout aims for doing 8 rounds of 15 kettlebell swings, 10 kettlebell clean and presses, and 5 kettlebell snatches as fast as you possibly can.


Starter Home Workouts to Put on Muscle Weight

At the gym, you have a wider variety of ways to put on muscle weight as to working out at home. Read them down below.



  • Chest, Triceps, and Shoulders Muscle Groups: Hitting these three together is time efficient as the muscles typically work together. Some exercises to start off include the bench press, the incline dumbbell press, the dumbbell chest flys, and rope tricep pushdowns.
  • Back and Biceps Muscle Groups: Working these two out together is also efficient, and some start exercises include horizantal pulls, lat pulldowns, standard curles, and hammer bicep curls.
  • Abdominal Muscle Groups:Some exercises that help build a strong core include hanging leg circles, ab rolls with side rotation, and hanging bicycles. These are all great starter exercises at the gym.
  • Leg Muscle Groups:Good starter exercises for legs in the gym include the barbell back squat, the front squat, the leg extension, calf raises, and the leg press.

Workout Plan

A good workout plan for starters is the push and pull days strategy. You switch between them every time you go to the gym, so that you can work everything out and not have to deal with your muscles being sore. Push days are when you push with your upper body, prioritizing chest, triceps, and shoulders. Pull days are ones where you pull with your upper body, prioritizing back and biceps. Leg/Ab days are the third day in this cycle, and as the name suggests you do abdominal and leg workouts on this day. Switching between the three every day and you got yourself a great workout plan!
