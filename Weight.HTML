Weight Management


Weight management is important to all of us. Whether you're looking to lose a few extra pounds, or put on muscle mass, we got you covered. Down below are some suggestions that will help you achieve your goals!



Weight Loss

Losing weight has a lot to do with the decisions you make every day. In order to achieve that goal, you have to eat well and train consinsistently.


What are some benefits of losing weight?

  • More self-esteem
  • Extra energy
  • Reduced blood pressure
  • Improves sleep
  • Reduced heartburn


Caloric Defecit

One of the most important factors in weight loss is being in a caloric defecit. This means to consume less calories than you burn every day. Here's how to reach a caloric deficit.

  • Avoid drinks with sugar
  • Eat less processed foods
  • Eat more fruits and vegetables
  • Eat more home-cooked meals
  • Try to reduce your daily caloric intake by 500

Whole foods

Eating whole foods allows you to better lower your caloric intake. Here's a few you can incorporate into your diet.

  1. Vegetables and fruits
  2. Fish
  3. Nuts and seeds
  4. Eggs and dairy
  5. Healthy starches

Exercise


Exercising is an important factor in weight loss. This helps you burn off even more unneccesary calories throughout the day. Running on a treadmill, biking, walking, and crutches are all great ways to help burn extra calories.

Muscle Gain



Muscle gaining typically incorporates being in a caloric surplus as well as exercising often. Down below are some suggestions to help you put on muscle mass.

Diet

  • Load up on and eat a lot of protein
  • Be in a caloric surplus, but avoid junk food.
  • Eat enough carbs to fuel your workouts

Exercises

  • Push ups and bench press: These two exercises focus on the triceps and chest muscles. There are many variations of them, and you can find out about them on our physical exercise page.
  • Squats and lunges: These exercises focus on your leg muscle growth. There are also many variation to these and they focus on different muscles in the legs.
  • Mixed martial arts: Taking any kind of mixed martial arts class will help you build muscle mass, as well as grow musclular endurance.
